# Country Expansion Plan

Country support is data-driven through ISO code, currency, languages, priority, capability coverage, provider eligibility, and regulatory status.

## Priorities

| Priority | Countries | Immediate capability |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Republic of Korea | Weather, currency, country information, geocoding, destination information; commercial connectors are placeholders |
| 2 | Japan, Vietnam, Thailand, Philippines, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Singapore | Same global public-data capability; localization/provider partnerships remain |
| 3 | United States, Canada, Australia, United Kingdom, France, Germany, Spain, Italy | Same global public-data capability; commercial/legal readiness remains |

## Country launch checklist

- Locale, date/time, currency, address, tax/fee display.
- Consumer, marketplace, travel, payments, privacy, accessibility, and minor protections.
- Provider contracts, permitted marks/data, pricing/cancellation display.
- Emergency, embassy, immigration, insurance, and regulated-service disclaimers.
- Local support/escalation and data-retention requirements.
- End-to-end tests for fallback and no-approval execution.

## Expansion effort

Adding a country to the technical catalog is under one day. Honest public-data validation and localization is 1–2 weeks. Commercial provider and legal readiness is typically 1–3 months per market and may run in parallel. No country is “commercially supported” merely because public weather or currency data works.
